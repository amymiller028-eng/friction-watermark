Praise for Friction:

“After decades in leadership development, I’ve read more books than I can count that tell leaders what they should do—without helping them navigate what actually happens. Friction is different. Ross Blankenship and Maggie Sass give leaders the language, insight, and practical tools to work with the emotional realities of leadership—not avoid them. It belongs on every leader’s desk.”

—Elaine Biech, CPTD Fellow, 2022 ISA Thought Leader, author of The Art and Science of Training

—Ken Blanchard, coauthor of The One Minute Manager and The Simple Truths of Leadership

“Ross and Maggie provide a simple, yet powerful framework for understanding what friction is in our lives and at work, how to respond to friction in ways that productively moves us and those around us forward, and, most importantly, they lay out a process to continuously learn through experimentation. If you are a new leader, one who has recently been promoted, or a seasoned executive, this book should be on your reading list.”

—Mark Blankenship, Chief of Staff & Strategy (retired), Jack-in-the-Box

“After more than a decade coaching leaders operating in stressful, high-growth environments, I’ve become convinced that emotional intelligence is central to effective leadership, and this book offers one of the most practical paths into that truth I’ve encountered. By reframing friction not as something to avoid, but as an unavoidable reality of life and organizations that must be worked with skillfully, Blankenship and Sass illuminate what emotional deftness looks like in practice. The second half of the book is especially valuable: a rich collection of experiments for navigating personal, interpersonal, and systemic friction that will expand any coach’s or leader’s toolkit. And at a moment when AI tempts us to sidestep more and more cognitive and emotional friction, Friction makes a compelling case that much of the discomfort we are eager to offload may be part of what keeps us human.”

—Andy Crissinger, Executive Coach, Worksong

“This is not a book about avoiding hard moments; it’s a book about using them. —Frances Frei, Harvard Business School Professor

Friction is exactly the kind of book that moves people from insight to action—which is where all real change begins.”
